Introduction

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was an Indian statesman, a senior leader of the Indian National Congress, and a founding father of the Republic of India. He played a leading role in the country's struggle for independence and guided its integration into a united, independent nation.

Known as the "Iron Man of India," Patel was a man of great wisdom and political insight. His efforts in uniting the 562 princely states into the Indian Union after independence were monumental. His leadership and vision helped shape the modern Indian nation.

Patel's legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians. His commitment to the nation, pragmatic approach to governance, and dedication to public service have made him an enduring symbol of Indian unity and strength.

Biography

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born on 31 October 1875 in Nadiad, Gujarat. He was the fourth of six children in his family. Patel exhibited leadership qualities from a young age and was known for his resilience and determination. After completing his primary education, he went on to study law in England, where he qualified as a barrister.

Upon returning to India, Patel became actively involved in the independence movement, inspired by the teachings of Mahatma Gandhi. He played a crucial role in organizing peasants in Gujarat to revolt against oppressive policies and practices of the British authorities. His leadership during the Bardoli Satyagraha in 1928 earned him the title "Sardar," meaning leader or chief.

Patel was a key figure in the Indian National Congress and worked closely with other leaders to strategize the fight for freedom. He was arrested multiple times by the British authorities but remained undeterred. His commitment to the cause of independence never wavered.

After India gained independence in 1947, Patel became the first Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Home Affairs. His most significant contribution during this period was the political integration of India. He successfully persuaded over 500 princely states to join the Indian Union, ensuring the country's unity and stability.

Patel's efforts in building a strong administrative foundation for the newly independent nation were instrumental. He was also responsible for establishing the All India Services, which laid the groundwork for a unified civil service system in India.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel passed away on 15 December 1950, leaving behind a legacy of unity, integrity, and nation-building. His contributions to India are commemorated through various institutions and the Statue of Unity, which stands as a tribute to his enduring legacy.

Words to the Nation

"Every citizen of India must remember that he is an Indian and he has every right in this country but with certain duties."

"Manpower without unity is not a strength unless it is harmonized and united properly, then it becomes a spiritual power."

"My only desire is that India should be a good producer and no one should be hungry, shedding tears for food in the country."

His life is a testament to the power of perseverance and determination. Sardar Patel's contributions to the formation of modern India make him a role model for generations to come.

Statue of Unity

The Statue of Unity is a colossal statue of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, located in the state of Gujarat, India. It is the world's tallest statue, standing at a height of 182 meters (597 feet). The statue was inaugurated on 31 October 2018, the 143rd birth anniversary of Sardar Patel, by the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi.

The statue is situated on the Narmada River, facing the Sardar Sarovar Dam. It was designed by Indian sculptor Ram V. Sutar and constructed by Larsen & Toubro, a leading Indian engineering company. The project was completed in a record time of 33 months.

The Statue of Unity is a tribute to Sardar Patel's contributions to the nation, particularly his role in the integration of the princely states into independent India. The statue symbolizes the values of unity, patriotism, and national integrity that Patel stood for.

The site also includes a museum and exhibition hall dedicated to Sardar Patel's life and achievements, an observation deck at 153 meters, which offers a panoramic view of the surrounding area, and a research center for agriculture development and tribal welfare.
